Type of tax and how to calculate them

Land development tax

What is land development tax?

Land development tax is a tax that the government collected on lands that have man-made structures on it. This tax include, but not limited to: houses, shops, schools, restaurant, and tourist attractions. All new developments will get taxed, but you may file for tax return on exempted builds or lands.

How to calculate land development tax

Land development tax will be charged on build to build basis.

Step 1: determine your bracket
Your bracket is a variable to decided on the percentage of your tax. Bracket can be calculate by divide the area of your development to the highest bracket. If the bracket you calculated is greater than 1 they your bracket will be 1.
Step 2: determine your tax percentage
Your tax percentage can be calculate from the bracket by getting the highest tax percentage multiply the bracket raised to the power of tax exponent. The government will inform highest tax percentage and tax exponent when you apply for development ID.
Step 3: determine your tax
A fixed rate will be charged for every new development. When apply for development ID, an inspector will arrive at the development and assess the value per block of the developed area. The tax on that development will be the value of the development multiply by the tax percent plus the fixed rate. A receipt will be send along with your development ID, and the deadline for paying will also be specified on that receipt.
